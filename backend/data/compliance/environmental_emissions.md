## 🚨 Environmental Incidents & Exceedances (2026 YTD)

### ENV-2026-001: Flaring Incident with Visible Smoke
- **Date/Time**: 2026-03-22 | 18:45 (Duration: 15 mins)
- **Unit**: Flare System
- **Detected By**: Visual Observation / CCTV
- **Investigator**: Aisha Tariq Khan (ENG-009)
- **Description**: A sudden upset in the CDU unit (caused by a false level transmitter reading on LIT-103-001) resulted in a massive dump of hydrocarbon gas to the flare header. The flare assist steam control valve could not open fast enough to provide smokeless combustion.
- **Environmental Impact**: Black smoke (Ringelmann No.3) visible outside plant boundaries for 15 minutes.
- **Corrective Action**: Tuning of the flare steam control logic (implemented by Priyanka Verma, ENG-005).

### ENV-2026-002: FCC Particulate Matter Exceedance
- **Date/Time**: 2026-05-18 | 10:00 - 14:00 (Duration: 4 hrs)
- **Unit**: FCC Regenerator Stack
- **Detected By**: CEMS Analyzer Alert
- **Investigator**: Aisha Tariq Khan (ENG-009) & Dineshkumar Shah (ENG-004)
- **Description**: PM emissions spiked to 85.2 mg/Nm³ (Limit: 50). 
- **Root Cause**: Failure of one of the Electrostatic Precipitator (ESP) fields due to a snapped discharge wire.
- **Action Taken**: Load reduced on FCC to bring emissions back within limits. ESP field repaired online during a short maintenance window by Jayaram Srinivasan (ELEC-001).
- **Regulatory Action**: Exceedance reported to GPCB within 24 hours as per compliance protocol. No fine levied due to prompt action.
